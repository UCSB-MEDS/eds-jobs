FISH & WILDLIFE BIOLOGIST 3 


WILDLIFE DIVERSITY SPECIES DATA BIOLOGIST 


WDFW Wildlife Program 
Olympia, Washington 


The preferred duty station is Olympia, Washington, however, we are also considering other duty stations within Washington State.
You are excited to support diversity species biologists by managing species data collected from field surveys, telemetry projects, other remotely sensed observations, and public reports of wildlife observations! 
You thrive on building collaboration and teamwork – become the liaison between the Wildlife-Science Division’s IT, research staff, business units, Diversity Division staff and other WDFW employees that utilize diversity  species data.
Join a team of IT Staff, GIS Analysts, and Data Stewards that provide the tools, services, and products to support the entire data management lifecycle for diversity species data.
With that in mind, 
Picture yourself being responsible for quality assurance, quality control, management, interpretation, usability, delivery, and supporting the analyses of diversity species data.
In this dynamic role you will improve the quantity and quality of wildlife diversity species data by soliciting, acquiring, translating, and processing survey, research, incidental, and external species data.
The optimal applicants have strong organizational skills, have the willingness and aptitude to manage diversity species data, and provide excellent customer service to internal and external partners.  
We are seeking a candidate with demonstrated values matching those of WDFW: Accountability, Service, Professionalism, Integrity, Respect, and Empathy (ASPIRE).


Duties
Our Wildlife Diversity Species Data Biologist,
Manage diversity Species of Greatest Conservation Need (SGCN) data in corporate databases.  Tasks include but are not limited to: 
* Solicits and acquires species data from WDFW staff and external entities.
* Conducts quality control, translation and processing of species data and incorporates this data into WDFW datasets and enterprise species databases.
* Uses or modifies scripts and other automated geo-processing routines for managing data and maintains/updates existing species records in corporate databases.
* Liaises with WDFW staff and species work groups to identify business needs for species data and aligning species biology with species data business rules.
Support species surveys and other field data collection by WDFW staff.  Tasks include but are not limited to: 
* Coordinates with biologists to develop survey data collection procedures, such as survey protocols and mapping criteria, and to align survey protocols with species data business rules, to improve the flow of survey data into corporate species databases. 
* Coordinates with Wildlife Program IT staff on the development of surveys apps, tools, and scripts for appropriately collecting, monitoring and QC-ing of survey data. 
* Supports the use of platforms and systems for managing data collected from camera traps and other indirect species data collection surveys such as telemetry, acoustic recordings, or drone imagery.
Support species evaluations, assessments, planning, reporting by WDFW staff.   Tasks include but are not limited to: 
* Coordinates with biologists to determine business needs, identify data gaps and solicit data from internal and external sources.
* Compiles and standardizes data for integration into species databases and use in species evaluations.

Qualifications
REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS:
* A Bachelor's degree in wildlife management, environmental science, biology, or a related natural resource discipline. 
AND 
One (1) of the following:
* Two (2) years as a Fish & Wildlife Biologist 2. 
OR  
* Three (3) years of professional experience in management, field-technician or research jobs involving wildlife, habitats, forestry, or similar biological, environmental, and natural resources.
Please Note: 
* Closely related qualifying experience may be substituted for the required education on a year-by-year basis.
* A Master’s degree in the applicable science discipline will substitute for one (1) year of the required experience. 
* A Ph.D. in the applicable science may be substituted for two (2) years of the required experience.
Certifications/Licenses: 
* Valid State Driver’s License. 
PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS: 
Experience with:
* Using GIS software such as ArcPro or ArcGIS for Desktop and using this software for geodatabase management, geoprocessing, and spatial analyses.
* Intermediate experience Microsoft Office software, including Access databases.
* Statistical software and understanding of statistical methods for evaluating species, species populations, and species-habitat relationships.
Knowledge of: 
* Principles of conservation biology and wildlife management.
Familiarity with: 
* Washington state species’ habitats, ranges, and distribution.
* Standard biological field methods, survey protocols, and survey tools for observing and collecting species data.
Ability to: 
* Effectively identify, collect, organize, and document data in ways that make the information most useful for subsequent assessment, analysis, and investigation.
Knowledge of, or experience with: 
* Relational databases and data management processes within those databases.
* Managing data on cloud platforms and software such as ArcGIS.com, Amazon Web Services, or Azure
* Managing hardcopy and electronic records
* Scripting languages of Python and/or R for automating data management.
Resume must indicate experience in these requirements and years of experience.
